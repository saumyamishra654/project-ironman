# Half Marathon Plan — Delhi, 18 Oct 2026

**Race:** Half Marathon (21.1 km), Delhi, Sunday 18 Oct 2026 — hot, likely 24–28°C at race pace.
**Goal:** 6:00/km (2:06:36) as the *stretch ceiling*. **Race-day plan: 6:10–6:15/km, negative split**, drop to 6:00 only if halfway feels easy and it's cool.
**Runway:** 14 weeks (starting week of Mon 13 Jul). Race falls on the Sunday of Week 14.

---

## Reality check (why the pacing above)

- Riegel off a 30-min 5k → HM ≈ **2:18 (6:32/km)**; off a 1:04 10k → **≈2:21 (6:41/km)**. Your *current* engine is ~6:30–6:40 HM pace.
- 6:00/km currently sits at your **threshold** (tempo zone 5:50–6:05). The plan's job is to lower threshold so 6:00 becomes *comfortably sub-threshold* and raceable for 21 km.
- Upside: as a swimmer with a strong aerobic base but little run-specific distance, you'll likely **beat Riegel** once the long run is built — the endurance is the missing piece, not the engine.
- Heat: Delhi + no acclimation in Bengaluru ≈ **−15–20 sec/km** on the day. Bank fitness for 6:00; race 6:10–6:15.

> **Week 1 action:** run a 5k *or* 10k time trial (cool morning, fresh) and recompute paces off the real number. Everything below assumes your current zones — adjust if the TT says otherwise.

## Structure

Four runs/week, slotted into your existing week. Keep your 2 swims (great for HIM + low-impact aerobic) and lifts, but **protect the long run and quality run** — don't stack a heavy leg-lifting session the day before either.

- **R1 — Long run (Sunday):** the spine. Mostly easy; goal-pace blocks added in the back half of the block.
- **R2 — Quality (midweek, e.g. Tue):** threshold → VO2 → goal-pace, periodized.
- **R3 — Midweek easy (e.g. Thu):** lengthened aerobic run. Your old "short easy" run does the least work — this is where free volume lives.
- **R4 — Recovery + strides (e.g. Fri/Sat):** genuinely easy, 5–6 km, finish with 4–6 × 20s strides for economy. Skip if legs are cooked.

## Weekly layout (4 runs + 1 swim + 3 lifts)

**Constraints:** uppers ≥2–3 days apart; no 3-run streaks; run adjacencies only as quality→easy; the one required double ideally short-easy+upper or short-easy+swim; long run Sunday, never adjacent to another run (so Sat and Mon are run-free).

**Why this shape is forced:** 3 remaining runs into Tue–Fri with no triple gives only two patterns, and the adjacency rule (quality first) plus the lower lift needing a non-compromising slot eliminates one. The surviving family: **Quality Tue → short easy Wed, longer easy Fri**, with Thursday as the clean lower-lift pocket (between easy runs, 2 after quality, 3 before the long run).

### Chosen layout (A1)

| Day | Session | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Swim | Non-impact flush after the long run. |
| **Tue** | Quality run | 2 days after the long run — the one unavoidable pinch (see below). |
| **Wed** | Short easy run + strides (R4) **+ Upper A** | The double: recovery flush after quality + the non-bench upper day. Easy to time. |
| **Thu** | Lower lift | The clean pocket: shin-safe, between easy runs, 3 days clear of the long run. Calf work here. |
| **Fri** | Longer easy run (R3) | Standalone — the harder run to time gets its own day. |
| **Sat** | Upper B (bench day) | Leg-free day before the long run; bench on rested pressing muscles. |
| **Sun** | Long run | Fully isolated: upper-only Sat before, swim Mon after. |

- **Run adjacency:** only Tue→Wed, quality→short-easy — exactly the allowed pairing. No triples.
- **Uppers:** Wed & Sat, 3 days apart. Make Sat the bench/push-priority day (progression lives there), Wed the other.
- **The known pinch:** quality sits 2 days after the long run. The enumeration shows no layout moves it further without breaking another constraint — something must sit at 2 days, and this puts the pinch on the *post*-long side (recoverable) rather than pre-long. If Tuesday quality is consistently flat, trim Sunday's long run slightly rather than reshuffle.

### Alternatives (same family, different swim/upper placement)

- **A2 — max upper spacing:** Mon Upper A, Tue Quality, Wed short easy + Swim, Thu Lower, Fri longer easy, Sat Upper B, Sun Long. Uppers 5 days apart (best for bench), double = short easy + swim. Works because uppers are leg-neutral, so Monday-after-long-run is a fine *upper* slot. Cost: no Monday recovery swim. Switch to this if the bench progression starts mattering more than leg freshness (e.g., post-race block).
- **A3 — Saturday double:** as A2 but Swim moves to Sat with Upper B. Weakest: double isn't a preferred pairing and the swim eats into pre-long-run recovery.

**No dedicated rest day (your call), caveat stands:** 7 days on; Mon (swim) and Sat (upper-only) are the pressure valves — convert one to true rest if fatigue or shins build. Wk 4 / Wk 8 down weeks don't replace week-to-week reading.

**Shin escape hatch:** if shins niggle, cut *running impact*, not lifting — swap Wed's short run (or Fri's longer easy) for an easy spin/swim. Same aerobic volume, zero tibial pounding.

## Phase map

- **Wks 1–4 — Threshold base:** raise the sustainable-pace ceiling. Continuous/broken tempo.
- **Wks 5–8 — Add VO2:** raise the ceiling with 3-min hard reps, alternating with threshold.
- **Wks 9–12 — HM-specific:** goal-pace blocks in quality + long runs. Teach 6:00 economy.
- **Wks 13–14 — Taper:** cut volume, keep a little sharpness, arrive fresh.

Recovery weeks at **Wk 4 and Wk 8** (down long run, easier quality) — non-negotiable. For shin-prone runners the periodic drop in impact volume is the single best defence against tibial stress building up unnoticed.

## Heat & race-day pacing

- **Acclimate:** in Weeks 12–13, do 2–3 runs in the warmest part of the day (or overdress) to nudge heat adaptation. Bengaluru won't do this for you automatically.
- **Race plan:** first 5 km at **6:15**, settle to **6:10** through halfway. If you're comfortable and it's cool, drop toward 6:00 for the last 8 km. **Negative split beats fading** — going out at 6:00 in Delhi heat is how you walk from km 16.
- **Fuel/hydrate:** practice race-morning breakfast and mid-run hydration on the Week 10 and 12 long runs. Take fluid at every station on race day.

## Shin & load management (tibial stress risk)

Shin issues are an **impact-and-volume** problem, so the levers differ from a tendon problem:

- **Mileage ramp is the main risk.** Tibial bone adapts slower than muscle/fitness, so a too-fast jump in weekly running volume is the classic trigger. The plan already caps long-run growth (**never jump >1.5 km week-on-week** to "catch up") and drops volume at Wk 4 / Wk 8 — take those down weeks fully.
- **Cadence check.** Overstriding drives up tibial impact. Pull your average cadence off the Garmin; if it's well under ~170–175 spm, nudge it up 5% on easy runs — it's one of the cheapest ways to cut shin load without losing pace.
- **Prehab (2×/week):** soleus is the priority — it absorbs a large share of tibial impact when you run, so **bent-knee (soleus) calf raises** alongside straight-leg gastroc raises, plus **tibialis-anterior raises** and heel walks. Loaded calf work on your lower-lift day counts.
- **Surface & shoes:** favour softer surfaces for easy runs where you can, and track shoe mileage (dead midsoles quietly raise impact). Strides on grass.
- **Early warning:** *diffuse* ache along the inner shin = manageable MTSS — back off volume, add calf work. **Pinpoint** tibial pain that worsens through a run or lingers next day = possible bone stress: stop running, cross-train, get it looked at. Don't run through pinpoint pain.
- **First response to a flare:** cut *running impact*, not everything. Swap runs for bike/swim to hold aerobic fitness while the tibia settles — you can maintain most of your engine off the bike for a week or two if needed.
- **Taper (Wks 13–14):** reduced running volume is protective here, so this phase works in your favour. Keep lower-lift volume light too, mainly to arrive fresh.

## Thursday lower session (~65–70 min)

One leg day/week during a run block: enough to maintain strength and build shin/tendon capacity, light enough to leave Friday's easy run and Sunday's long run intact. **Loads stay at RPE 7–8 — leave 2 reps in the tank.** This is not a strength-PR block; grinding sets here taxes Sunday.

Balance check: 4 posterior movements (RDL, SL RDL, leg curl, Copenhagen) vs 3 anterior (Zercher, split squat, sissy) — deliberately posterior-tilted, since hamstrings do heavy work in late swing phase and are the classic undertrained running muscle.

| # | Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Zercher squat** | 3 × 6–8 | Main squat. If forearms/biceps limit before legs, swap to front squat or leg press. |
| 2 | **Barbell RDL** | 3 × 8 | Heaviest posterior movement — the SL version is balance-capped, this is where hamstrings actually get *loaded*. |
| 3 | **Bulgarian split squat** | 3 × 8/leg | Knee-dominant unilateral. |
| 4 | **Single-leg RDL** | 3 × 8/leg | Hip-dominant unilateral — every week, no alternation. |
| 5 | **Sissy squat** | 2–3 × 8–12 | **Progress slowly:** assisted + partial range weeks 1–4, then earn depth. First exercise to regress if the knee ever murmurs. |
| 6 | **Leg curl (seated if available)** | 2–3 × 10–12 | Knee-flexion function of the hamstrings — otherwise untrained. Seated > lying (long muscle length). **Nordics deferred to post-race:** better exercise, but the introductory DOMS wrecks long runs mid-block. |
| 7 | **Straight-leg calf raise** | 3 × 10–12, slow 3s lowering | Gastroc. Add load once 12 is easy. |
| 8 | **Bent-knee (seated/soleus) calf raise** | 3 × 12–15 | **The shin-protection exercise** — soleus absorbs the biggest share of tibial load in running. Do not skip. |
| 9 | **Tibialis raises** | 3 × 15–20 | Wall tib raises or loaded. Anterior shin balance. |
| 10 | **Copenhagen plank** | 2 × 20–30s/side | Adductors — most-neglected running muscle. Short lever first. |

**First-week expectation:** Friday's easy run will feel heavy the first time you run the full version — judge the session by how *Sunday* feels, not Friday. If the long run degrades two weeks running, cut in this order: leg curl sets → RDL to 2 sets. Never cut the calf/tib block — that's injury-prevention work, not volume work.

**Down weeks (4, 8) and taper (13–14):** cut to exercises 1, 2, 7, 8, 9 at 2 sets each. Calf/tib work continues through the taper — it's protective, not fatiguing.

## Daily stretch/mobility routine (~10 min)

Same short sequence every day, ideally evenings or post-session. Consistency beats duration — 10 min daily outperforms 40 min twice a week. Holds ~45–60s per side unless noted.

1. **Wall calf stretch, straight knee** — gastroc.
2. **Wall calf stretch, bent knee** — soleus. The pair matters for shins and for ankle dorsiflexion (which also cleans up squat depth).
3. **Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch** — squeeze the glute of the down leg; counteracts sitting + running tightness.
4. **Standing/lying hamstring stretch** — supports the SL RDL pattern.
5. **Couch stretch or standing quad stretch** — quads/rectus femoris; also feeds sissy squat range.
6. **90/90 hip rotations** — ~1 min flowing; internal + external hip rotation.
7. **Ankle dorsiflexion rocks** — knee-over-toe rocks, 10–15/side; ankle stiffness quietly raises tibial load.
8. **Down-dog → cobra flow** — 5 slow cycles; posterior chain + thoracic, closes the routine.

On run days, items 1, 2, 7 (calves + ankles) are the non-negotiables if you're short on time — they're the shin-relevant third of the list.

## Calibration

Re-baseline paces after the Week-1 TT, and again if a long-run goal-pace block feels wildly easy or hard.
