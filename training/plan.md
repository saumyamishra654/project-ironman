## Warm-Up Protocols

### Lifting (all sessions)

1. **General (5 min):** jump rope, rowing machine, or brisk walk
2. **Specific:** ramp to working weight on the first compound lift
   - Empty bar × 10
   - 50% working weight × 5
   - 70% working weight × 3
   - 85% working weight × 1–2
   - Begin working sets

No static stretching before lifting.

### Quality Run (Wednesday)

1. 5 min walk
2. 5 min very easy jog (~8:00+ /km)
3. Dynamic stretches: leg swings front-back and lateral (10 each), hip circles (5 each direction), walking lunges (5 each leg), high knees (20 sec), butt kicks (20 sec)
4. 2–3 min easy jog
5. Begin session

**Cool-down:** 5 min easy jog → 5 min walk → stretch calves, hip flexors, quads (30 sec each)

### Long Run (Sunday)

1. 5 min walk
2. First 1–2 km at 8:00+ /km — this IS the warm-up
3. Settle into target pace by km 2–3

**Cool-down:** walk until HR drops below 120. Stretch hip flexors, quads, calves, hamstrings (30–45 sec each).

### Easy Run (Saturday, weeks 13+)

No formal warm-up. Walk 2 min, start jogging.

### Swimming

**Warm-up:** 200 m easy freestyle.
**Cool-down:** 100–200 m easy backstroke.

### Cycling

**Warm-up:** 5 min easy spinning (low resistance, 85+ rpm).
**Cool-down:** 5 min easy spinning.

Do before bed or after dinner. Floor-based, no equipment needed.

| Exercise | Duration / Reps | Notes |

| 90/90 Hip Switches | 10 each side | Sit tall, rotate smoothly |
| Pigeon Stretch | 60 sec each side | Keep hips square |
| Couch Stretch (hip flexor) | 60 sec each side | Squeeze glute of rear leg |
| Standing Hamstring Stretch | 60 sec each side | Foot on chair, hinge at hips, flat back |
| Supine Figure-4 Stretch | 60 sec each side | Targets deep external rotators |
| Deep Squat Hold | 60 sec | Heels down, elbows push knees out |

No bouncing, no forcing range. Consistency beats depth — 10 min every night beats 30 min twice a week.

## Plyometric Progression

Total contacts per session: 10–30. All plyo work performed fully fresh before any other lifting. Stop a set the moment ground contact time increases or jump height drops.

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Mechanics | 1–8 | Landing quality, stiffness, low-amplitude work |
| Reactivity | 9–16 | Bounds, aggressive rebound, elastic loading |
| Intensity | 17–24 | Maximal intent, faster contacts, lower total volume |
| Taper | 25–27 | Maintain with reduced volume |

## Recovery Rules

1. **If only one session can happen:** prioritise PM cardio on Wed/Sun (running is primary). All other days, prioritise AM lift.
2. **Fatigue triage order (cut first → last):**
   - Tuesday Z2 cycle
   - Saturday threshold cycle → convert to Z2
   - Lifting accessories (keep main lifts)
   - Saturday easy run
   - Swimming volume (shorten, don't skip)
   - **Never cut:** Long run, Quality run, Bench press
3. **If knees flare:** cut plyometrics first, reduce cycling, keep swimming.
4. **If bench stalls 3+ weeks:** check sleep and protein. If both solid, add close-grip bench on Friday temporarily.
5. **If a long run goes badly (bonk, injury scare, RPE 9+):** next week's long run stays same or drops.
6. **Post-marathon (weeks 28–29):** easy aerobic only. No running until easy jogging feels easy. No heavy lifting until week 29.

## Marathon Race Strategy

**Target:** Completion. Realistic finish: 4:15–4:45 (flat), 4:35–5:10 (hilly/Mussoorie).

- Go out at 6:15–6:20 /km for the first half
- Do not go faster than 6:00 /km in the first 10 km regardless of how good you feel
- Walk aid stations if needed
- If the first half felt controlled, hold pace through 30 km, then survive
- Race week: sleep, hydrate, carb load 2–3 days before. Nothing done in race week improves fitness.
